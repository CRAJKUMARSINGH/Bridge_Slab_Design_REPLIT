What is Afflux?

Afflux is the rise in water level upstream of a bridge structure caused by the obstruction to flow created by the bridge piers, abutments, and deck slab. It's a critical parameter in bridge design to ensure the structure can safely accommodate flood flows.

Why Afflux Matters

Understanding afflux is essential for:

  • Determining the required deck elevation
  • Ensuring adequate clearance during flood events
  • Preventing backwater effects on upstream areas
  • Ensuring safe passage of debris and floating objects

Afflux Calculation Process

Methodology

The calculation follows IS: 7784 (Part-I) 1975 using the Molesworth Formula:

Afflux h = ((V²/17.85) + 0.0152) × (A²/a² - 1)

Where:

  • h = afflux in meters
  • V = velocity in the unobstructed stream (m/s)
  • A = unobstructed sectional area of the river (m²)
  • a = obstructed sectional area at the bridge (m²)

Key Parameters Considered

  • Unobstructed Area (A): 490.30 m²
  • Obstructed Area (a): 337.92 m²
  • Stream Velocity (V): 1.84 m/s
  • Bridge Configuration: 12 spans of 8.00m each
  • Pier Obstruction: 11 piers of 1.20m width

Detailed Calculation

Calculation Results

Calculated Afflux: 0.23 meters

Key Results

  • Afflux Height: 0.23 m
  • Afflux Flood Level: 100.83 m
  • Deck Slab Top Level: 101.60 m
  • Clearance: 0.77 m above afflux level
  • Obstructed Velocity: 2.67 m/s

Result Interpretation

The calculated afflux of 0.23 meters indicates a relatively minor impact on the upstream water level. With the deck slab positioned at 101.60m and the afflux flood level at 100.83m, there is adequate clearance of 0.77 meters, ensuring:

  • No hindrance to traffic during high floods
  • Sufficient freeboard for safety
  • Proper hydraulic performance of the structure

Engineering Conclusion

Design Verification: PASS

The afflux calculation confirms that the bridge design meets all hydraulic requirements:

  • Afflux is within acceptable limits (0.23m)
  • Adequate clearance between deck and flood level
  • Safe velocity conditions (2.67 m/s)
  • No risk of upstream flooding

Design Recommendations

  1. Maintain current deck elevation of 101.60m
  2. Ensure proper debris management during flood seasons
  3. Monitor upstream water levels during extreme events
  4. Consider future sedimentation effects in long-term maintenance
